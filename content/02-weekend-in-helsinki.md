<p class="lead">
Two to three days is plenty for a first visit to Helsinki — a compact, walkable city on the sea with great design, serious coffee, and a sauna culture you shouldn't skip.
</p>

## Day by day

<div class="steps">
  <div class="step">
    <div class="step-content">
      <strong>Day 1 — Senate Square & the waterfront</strong>
      <p>Start at Senate Square and the Helsinki Cathedral, then walk down to Market Square. Browse the Old Market Hall for local produce, smoked fish, and Finnish pastries. In the evening, book a public sauna — Allas Sea Pool or Löyly are both excellent and right on the water.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="step">
    <div class="step-content">
      <strong>Day 2 — Suomenlinna & Design District</strong>
      <p>Take the 15-minute HSL ferry to Suomenlinna, a UNESCO sea fortress spread across several islands. Bring a picnic and spend a few hours exploring the ramparts. Back in the city, walk the Design District — tight streets packed with Finnish design shops, galleries, and cafés.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="step">
    <div class="step-content">
      <strong>Day 3 — Temppeliaukio & Kallio</strong>
      <p>Visit Temppeliaukio Church (the Rock Church), carved directly into bedrock — worth it even if you're not into churches. Then head to Kallio, the neighbourhood with the best bars, record shops, and independent restaurants. The Sunday flea market at Hietalahdentori is a great finale.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

## Getting around

<div class="kv-list">
  <div class="kv-row">
    <span class="kv-key">Airport to centre</span>
    <span class="kv-value">Ring Rail Line — about 30 min, covered by HSL day ticket</span>
  </div>
  <div class="kv-row">
    <span class="kv-key">City transport</span>
    <span class="kv-value">HSL app or day ticket — covers trams, buses, metro & local ferries</span>
  </div>
  <div class="kv-row">
    <span class="kv-key">Suomenlinna ferry</span>
    <span class="kv-value">Market Square pier, every 30–60 min, included in HSL ticket</span>
  </div>
  <div class="kv-row">
    <span class="kv-key">Taxis & rideshare</span>
    <span class="kv-value">Bolt is available but Helsinki is very walkable — mostly unnecessary</span>
  </div>
</div>

## Good to know

<div class="callout callout-info">
  <h4>Tip</h4>
  <p>Book your sauna slot in advance — Löyly and Allas Sea Pool fill up fast on weekends, especially in summer. Evenings on the water are worth planning around.</p>
</div>

<div class="callout callout-warning">
  <h4>Watch out</h4>
  <p>Many smaller shops and restaurants close early on Sundays or don't open at all. Plan museum visits and sit-down dinners for Friday or Saturday.</p>
</div>

## Worth it?

<div class="two-col">
  <div class="col col-green">
    <h4>Highlights</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>Compact and easy to walk or tram around</li>
      <li>Design and architecture are genuinely world-class</li>
      <li>Sauna culture is a one-of-a-kind experience</li>
      <li>Suomenlinna justifies the trip on its own</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="col col-red">
    <h4>Things to know</h4>
    <ul>
      <li>One of the pricier capitals in Europe</li>
      <li>Dark and cold outside of summer</li>
      <li>Nightlife is quieter than other Nordic cities</li>
      <li>Sunday closures can catch you off guard</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>
